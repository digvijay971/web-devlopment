Founding and History Founder: Lord Baden-Powell established the movement in England in 1907, starting with a trial camp for boys on Brownsea Island.

India: The Scout movement was introduced in India in 1909, with the Girl Guide movement starting in 1911. International Scope: Scouting and Guiding is a voluntary, non-political, secular, and non-sectarian international organization spread across many countries, with tens of millions of members worldwide. Purpose and Objectives Character Development: To build strong character by inculcating values of discipline, self-control, and patriotism. Membership and Structure Age Sections: The movement has different sections for various age groups, including: Bunny: (3-5 years) Cub/Bulbul: (6-10 years) Scout/Guide: (11-17 years) Rover/Ranger: (18-25 years)
